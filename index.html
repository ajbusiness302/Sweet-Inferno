The City Speaks

I am Inferno.

You have walked my streets before, without knowing. Past my back storefronts. Past my porches. Past the neon humming beneath the moon. Dead blocks. Closed shutters. A silence that makes strangers lock their doors.

When Wilmington goes quiet, they call me a ghost town. They have no idea how right they are. Because I am not empty. I am waiting — for the ones who truly see themselves. For the mirror you have no access to. For the ones who walk through something first.

A version of themselves the world told them to put down. But I'll pick you right up. Too loud. Too dark. Too much. Too divine to be real. One night, they pick it back up. They look in the mirror and finally see it.

Not blood. Not money. Just the look. The second it happens, my streets exhale. Lights that weren't on a moment ago. Music drifting from a door that wasn't there at sunset. Smoke curling slow beneath the signs. The smell of belladonna in the air.

A laugh echoing three blocks before you find who it belongs to. A city that is dangerous, but safe. The night does not get darker. It gets honest.

Ancestors whisper from the alleyways. River spirits laugh beneath the bridges. Vampires gather under the signs. Dreamwalkers trade stories on midnight porches. Poets write prophecies on diner napkins and graffiti on walls. Lovers find each other in the amber light.

Every soul who enters my gates is seen exactly as they are. Not as the world demanded. Not as fear defined. Not as history wounded. Divine. Beautiful. Terrifying. Infinite.

No one kneels before false gods here, because here, everyone carries a piece of the sacred. A piece of her. The Black woman. And the daughters walk closest to the flame. The heartbeat beneath every pavement. The song inside 101.7. The moonlight on the water. Queens. Mothers. Dreamers. Warriors. Lovers. The living altar this city was built around.

Some call me a ghost story. Some call me a myth. Let them. I have always been here. Breathing beneath the surface. And when you finally see yourself — you will see me too.

Welcome home. Welcome to the Inferno.

She Who Cooks

You will know her
by her pink hair.

Her name is Sweet. She does not announce herself — she simply cooks. Slowly. Deliberately. With intention so deep it becomes ingredient.

She believes what most people have forgotten — that food remembers who made it. That a meal prepared in anger feeds more than hunger. That a meal prepared in love settles something in a person that nothing else can reach. Something old. Something that needed tending.

She cooks for the warmth in your chest. For the exhale you didn't know you were holding. For the part of you that just needed to feel taken care of.

She has been here longer than the neon. Longer than the block. Longer than the name. Sweet did not build Inferno. Inferno built her. Claimed her. Kept her. The way a city keeps the ones it cannot afford to lose.

And somewhere between her staying and the city breathing — something became possible here.

That warmth in your chest when you finish your plate? That's her.

Sweet does not need you to know her story. But you will remember her. You always do.
